Origins

A History Beyond History

When San José State University was founded in 1857, the public record told one story. The Tower of the Timekeepers tells another.

For centuries before SJSU existed, a single unified wizard school operated in secrecy beneath the skin of ordinary reality — correcting timeline disruptions caused by powerful magic, war, invention, and human interference. As civilization accelerated, these disruptions became more frequent and more dangerous.

The Tower was established as the most secretive and advanced division within that school. Hidden inside what the world now knows as Tower Hall, it exists not in ordinary space but within a stabilized dimension anchored to the building — expansive, ancient, and completely invisible to the uninitiated.

"The majority of wizards will never know we exist. That is not failure — that is the mission."

The Tower's purpose is singular: to guard the integrity of history itself. Its students are trained to detect pivotal disruptions — moments where a single event could spiral into widespread ruin. When necessary, Timekeepers are permitted to intervene. Every action is calculated. Every consequence is carried.

Not every graduate has remained worthy. Some of the Tower's most gifted alumni — chosen for discipline and pure intent — eventually lost their way. Prolonged exposure to time magic altered their judgment. Their purpose shifted from service to control.

This is why the Tower does not simply train wizards. It forms them. Tests them. Watches them continuously — even after they leave.

The Tower does not recruit. It recognizes. If you are reading this, the observation period has already begun.

Location & Access

Finding the Tower

The entrance is accessed through Tower Hall on the campus of San José State University — a building that appears completely ordinary to the public. It is a wizard school hidden within another wizard school. The Silicon Valley skyline surrounds it. No one suspects a thing.

Faculty

Those Who Hold the Line

⧗
Dr. Tanja Nathanael
Dean of Towerism — Ph.D. in Daylight Savings — Ultimate Authority

She is not merely the leader of the Tower. She is the Tower. Dr. Nathanael holds the rarest power in existence: the ability to establish final versions of reality — determining which timeline becomes the true one. This power can be invoked only once in a lifetime, and she has not yet used it.

Beyond her temporal authority, she serves as Soul Seeker — able to read the hearts, spirits, and true intentions of every student before her. Initial evaluation is hers alone. Reexamination occurs every six months. No one enters the Tower without her recognition.

Professor Kevireon
Reality Anchoring

Students under Kevireon learn to stay rooted in themselves while the fabric of time bends around them. Advanced mindfulness, emotional control, and moral clarity are taught as survival skills — because without them, time magic will corrupt the best of intentions.

Prof. Ranius
Chronomantic Mastery

The combat division. Ranius teaches students to perceive an opponent's movements seconds before they happen, to accelerate the body, and to strike twice in the space of a single motion. This is time magic wielded as force — precise, disciplined, and devastating.

Prof. Uyendras
Chrono Cognization

Students trained by Uyendras gain the ability to navigate time as a visible field — perceiving all possible outcomes of a single decision simultaneously. The skill is extraordinary. The psychological weight is significant. Few are ready for what they see.

Prof. Dukrius
Paradox Prevention

The most consequential role in the Tower. Dukrius is responsible for detecting and neutralizing temporal paradoxes before they collapse entire timelines. Students in this track learn that the most dangerous threat to reality is not evil — it is a well-meaning mistake.

Curriculum

The Course of Time

All students begin with TIME 101. Entry into advanced coursework requires demonstrated mastery and faculty approval. Some courses carry access restrictions that cannot be negotiated.

TIME 101 — PREREQUISITE
Reality Anchoring

The foundation of everything. Students develop advanced mindfulness and moral clarity to resist the corrupting pull of time magic. You cannot progress without passing this course.

TIME 301
Advanced Reality Anchoring

Builds on TIME 101 with complex psychological scenarios drawn from actual timeline disruption events.

TIME 240 — PREREQUISITE
Chronomantic Mastery

Combat application of time magic. Perception, acceleration, precision. Taught only within the Tower.

TIME 340
Applied Chronomantic Combat

Field-level application of combat techniques with live simulations drawn from real crisis points.

TIME 210 — PREREQUISITE
Chrono Cognization

Students learn to navigate time as a visible field and perceive multiple decision outcomes simultaneously.

TIME 310
Predictive Timeline Analysis

Advanced training in reading timeline trajectories and forecasting points of critical instability.

TIME 280 — ELECTIVE
Memory & Timeline Stability

Trains students to preserve their identity when timelines shift — preventing confusion, memory loss, or psychological collapse.

TIME 275 — ELECTIVE
Temporal Ethics

Explores the moral consequences of altering time. Widely regarded as the most philosophically demanding course in the Tower.

TIME 290 — ELECTIVE
Multiversal Navigation

Introduces alternate timelines and parallel outcomes. Teaches students to navigate safely without becoming lost between realities.

TIME 330 — ELECTIVE
Crisis Point Intervention

Focuses on critical historical moments where intervention may be necessary — training students to act precisely and quickly under pressure.

TIME 350 — ELECTIVE
Controlled Time Travel Practicum

Supervised participation in limited timeline interventions. The closest students come to real deployment before graduation.

⚠ Restricted Access
TIME 401 — THESIS / MILESTONE
Paradox Prevention — The Tower

Elite. Invitation only. Students detect and neutralize temporal paradoxes that could collapse entire timelines. Only the most trusted are permitted entry. This is the capstone of everything the Tower stands for.

Who Is Welcome

Everyone Who Is Ready

The Tower does not sort by bloodline. It does not screen by origin, identity, or background. Magic is not something you are born into — it is something you commit to, develop, and choose to wield responsibly.

What the Tower looks for is simpler and rarer than any genetic gift: pure intent. The willingness to carry an enormous responsibility without using it for personal gain. The diversity of perspective, experience, and identity that our students bring is not a side effect of inclusion — it is central to solving problems that span all of human history.
